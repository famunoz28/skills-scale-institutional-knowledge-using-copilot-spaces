# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee and coordinate release cycles, ensuring all requirements are met before deployment. They manage rollout communications and handle incident and rollback procedures.

### Responsibilities
- Plan, schedule, and coordinate software releases
- Verify release readiness with development, QA, and operations teams
- Manage rollout communications and deployment documentation
- Coordinate incident response and rollback procedures if needed
- Maintain release notes and change logs

### Goals
- Ensure smooth, reliable, and on-schedule releases
- Minimize deployment risk and production incidents
- Maintain clear communication across teams during release events

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews and go/no-go meetings
- Deployment runbooks and release notes
- Post-deployment status updates to stakeholders

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Managers:** Coordinate on release scheduling, milestone tracking, and dependency management
- **Developers:** Confirm code freeze, deployment steps, and rollback procedures
- **Product Managers:** Align on feature completeness and release scope before go-live

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and refine business requirements, conduct gap analyses, translate stakeholder needs into actionable tasks, and track business-facing metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business requirements from stakeholders
- Conduct gap analyses between current and desired states
- Translate business needs into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Track and report on business-facing metrics and outcomes
- Facilitate requirement workshops and sign-off sessions

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet documented business goals and user needs
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or incomplete requirements
- Bridge communication between business stakeholders and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Business requirements documents (BRDs) and user stories
- Regular check-ins with Product Managers and Project Managers

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers:** Partner on backlog refinement and prioritization, aligning business goals with product strategy
- **Project Managers:** Provide requirement inputs for project plans and timelines, flag scope changes early
- **Developers:** Clarify acceptance criteria, answer requirement questions during implementation, and validate delivered solutions

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers design user flows, prototypes, and visual artifacts. They conduct user research and usability testing to ensure product interfaces meet user needs and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research, usability tests, and synthesize findings
- Establish and maintain design system components and standards
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria related to user experience
- Deliver design assets and specifications ready for development handoff

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible, and enjoyable product experiences
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in the development cycle
- Ensure design decisions are informed by real user data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs
- Research findings and usability reports
- Annotated design specs shared with developers

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers:** Collaborate on feature requirements and user stories, aligning design direction with product goals
- **Developers:** Provide detailed design specifications and participate in implementation reviews to ensure fidelity
- **Customer Success Lead:** Incorporate post-release user feedback into iterative design improvements

---

## Customer Success Lead

### Role Summary
Customer Success Leads serve as the main contact for user feedback and support issues. They track engagement, advocate for users, and relay actionable customer insights to the team.

### Responsibilities
- Collect, organize, and prioritize user feedback and support requests
- Monitor customer health metrics and engagement trends
- Advocate for user needs in backlog and roadmap discussions
- Coordinate with the team to resolve issues impacting customers
- Prepare customer-facing communications around releases and changes

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and product adoption
- Reduce time-to-resolution for user-reported issues
- Ensure the voice of the customer is represented in product decisions

### Typical Communication
- Regular syncs with Product Managers and Project Managers on top user issues
- Customer feedback summaries and engagement reports
- Release notes and user-facing announcements

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers:** Surface customer insights and adoption data to inform backlog prioritization and roadmap decisions
- **Project Managers:** Flag customer-impacting risks or issues that require escalation or schedule adjustment
- **Developers:** Relay reproducible bug reports and user-reported issues, provide context for prioritization
- **UX Designer:** Share qualitative feedback from users to guide iterative design improvements

## New Persona Integration Checklist

Use this checklist when adding a new persona to a project plan (e.g., during kickoff or planning):

- [ ] New persona(s) identified and role summaries reviewed with the team
- [ ] Responsibilities documented and no overlap with existing roles (or overlaps explicitly clarified)
- [ ] Handoff points and interaction patterns defined with adjacent roles
- [ ] Persona added to the project RACI/responsibility matrix
- [ ] Onboarding materials and relevant process docs shared with the new persona
- [ ] Communication patterns and meeting cadences agreed upon
- [ ] Acceptance criteria updated to reflect new persona's involvement where applicable
- [ ] Project plan and timeline reviewed for any impact from new persona's inclusion
